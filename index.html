What's Inside & Why
Sodium Citrate
The primary electrolyte lost through sweat. Sodium Citrate is the preferred pharmaceutical-grade sodium source — better tolerated than table salt, gentler on the gut, and superior at maintaining blood plasma volume and driving fluid absorption at the cellular level.
Potassium Citrate
Works in concert with sodium to regulate the body's fluid balance. The citrate form offers higher bioavailability and alkalising properties — helping buffer exercise-induced acidity while preventing muscle cramps and supporting cardiac function.
Calcium Citrate
Critical for muscle contraction, nerve signalling, and bone integrity. Calcium Citrate is absorbed significantly better than cheaper carbonate forms — regardless of whether you've eaten or not, your body gets what it needs.
Magnesium Chloride
Involved in over 300 enzymatic processes including ATP synthesis, muscle recovery, and protein building. Magnesium Chloride has one of the highest absorption rates of any magnesium compound — none of it goes to waste.
Citric Acid
Not just a flavour agent — citric acid plays a direct role in the Krebs cycle, your body's core energy production pathway. It also acts as a natural preservative and enhances mineral solubility in the formula.
Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
A potent antioxidant that neutralises the oxidative stress generated during intense physical activity. Vitamin C also supports immune function, collagen synthesis for joint health, and iron absorption — making it far more than a bonus ingredient.

What's Inside & Why
Dextrose (Glucose)
Activates the SGLT1 sodium-glucose co-transporter in your gut — the fastest known mechanism for simultaneous electrolyte and water absorption. This isn't sugar for taste. This is sugar as a delivery system, validated by decades of sports science research.
Sodium Chloride & Sodium Citrate
A dual sodium strategy. NaCl provides rapid ionic replenishment and triggers the thirst response that keeps you hydrating. Sodium Citrate adds alkalising buffering capacity to offset the lactic acid build-up during hard output — together they cover every angle of sodium physiology.
Magnesium Chloride
Highest bioavailability of any magnesium salt. MgCl₂ is absorbed rapidly and completely — supporting ATP energy production, muscle relaxation post-contraction, and nervous system recovery after intense sessions.
Potassium Chloride
Keeps your muscles firing in sync with your nervous system. Critical for sustained high-output activity, preventing the cellular potassium depletion that causes cramping and performance collapse in the late stages of exercise.
B-Vitamin Complex (B3, B6, B12)
Non-negotiable co-factors when carbohydrates are in the formula. B vitamins are the enzymes your mitochondria need to convert glucose into usable ATP. Without them, the dextrose in this formula is only half as effective. We put them in because we understand the biochemistry.
